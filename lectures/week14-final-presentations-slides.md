## Slide 7: Peer Feedback Guidelines | ピアフィードバックガイドライン

### How to Give Effective Feedback | 効果的なフィードバックの提供方法

#### DO: | すべきこと：
✅ **Be Specific** | 具体的に
- "The color contrast between text and background improves readability"
- Instead of "Good design"

✅ **Focus on Design, Not Personal Preferences** | 個人的な好みではなくデザインに焦点
- "This layout follows mobile-first principles effectively"
- Instead of "I like blue more than green"

✅ **Use HCI Principles** | HCI原則を使用
- "The navigation follows Jakob Nielsen's consistency heuristic"
- Reference course concepts

✅ **Balance Positive and Constructive** | 肯定的と建設的のバランス
- Start with strengths, then suggestions
- End on encouraging note

✅ **Be Respectful and Supportive** | 敬意を持ち支援的に
- Remember everyone worked hard
- Frame suggestions positively

#### DON'T: | すべきでないこと：
❌ **Don't Be Vague** | 曖昧にしない
- Avoid "It's fine" or "Looks good"

❌ **Don't Make It Personal** | 個人的にしない
- Focus on the design, not the designer

❌ **Don't Only Criticize** | 批判だけしない
- Include what works well

❌ **Don't Suggest Complete Redesigns** | 完全な再設計を提案しない
- Provide actionable, specific suggestions

❌ **Don't Compare to Other Students** | 他の学生と比較しない
- Each project is unique

## Slide 11: Ethical Considerations in Interface Design | インターフェースデザインにおける倫理的考慮事項

### Designing Responsibly | 責任を持ってデザインする

#### Privacy and Data Protection | プライバシーとデータ保護
- **Minimize Data Collection** | データ収集を最小化: Only collect what's necessary | 必要なものだけを収集
- **Transparent Practices** | 透明な実践: Tell users what data you collect and why | 何のデータを収集し、なぜなのかをユーザーに伝える
- **User Control** | ユーザーコントロール: Let users manage their data | ユーザーがデータを管理できるようにする
- **Secure Storage** | 安全な保管: Protect user information | ユーザー情報を保護

#### Inclusive and Accessible Design | 包括的でアクセス可能なデザイン
- **Design for All** | すべての人のためのデザイン: Consider diverse users from the start | 最初から多様なユーザーを考慮
- **WCAG Compliance** | WCAGコンプライアンス: Meet accessibility standards | アクセシビリティ基準を満たす
- **Cultural Sensitivity** | 文化的感受性: Respect different cultures and contexts | 異なる文化とコンテキストを尊重
- **Economic Accessibility** | 経済的アクセシビリティ: Don't exclude based on cost | コストに基づいて除外しない

#### Dark Patterns to Avoid | 避けるべきダークパターン
- **Deceptive Design** | 欺瞞的デザイン: Don't trick users into actions | ユーザーをアクションに誘導しない
- **Forced Continuity** | 強制継続: Make it easy to cancel subscriptions | サブスクリプションのキャンセルを簡単にする
- **Hidden Costs** | 隠れたコスト: Be transparent about pricing | 価格について透明に
- **Manipulative Urgency** | 操作的緊急性: Don't create false urgency | 偽の緊急性を作らない

#### Addictive Design Concerns | 中毒性デザインの懸念
- **Attention Economy** | 注意経済: Don't exploit psychological vulnerabilities | 心理的脆弱性を悪用しない
- **Infinite Scroll** | 無限スクロール: Consider user well-being | ユーザーの幸福を考慮
- **Notification Abuse** | 通知の悪用: Respect user attention | ユーザーの注意を尊重
- **Digital Well-being** | デジタルウェルビーイング: Help users manage their time | ユーザーが時間を管理するのを支援

#### Environmental Sustainability | 環境持続可能性

## Appendix: Detailed Rubric and Forms

### Full Presentation Rubric (35 points)

[See Slide 5 for summary]

**User Research (5 points)**
- 5: Exceptional depth, multiple methods, clear insights inform design
- 4: Strong research, clear findings, good connection to design
- 3: Adequate research, basic findings documented
- 2: Minimal research, weak connection to design
- 1: Little to no research evidence

**Design Process (5 points)**
- 5: Clear design thinking application, multiple iterations shown, well-justified decisions
- 4: Good process, some iterations, reasonable justifications
- 3: Basic process followed, limited iterations
- 2: Unclear process, few design decisions explained
- 1: No clear process demonstrated

**Final Prototype (5 points)**
- 5: Professional, polished, fully functional, exceeds requirements
- 4: High-quality, functional, meets requirements well
- 3: Adequate quality, mostly functional, meets basic requirements
- 2: Basic prototype, limited functionality
- 1: Incomplete or non-functional prototype

**Usability Testing (5 points)**
- 5: Thorough testing, clear methodology, actionable insights, iterations shown
- 4: Good testing, clear findings, some iterations
- 3: Basic testing conducted, some findings documented
- 2: Minimal testing, limited findings
- 1: Little to no testing evidence

**Accessibility (5 points)**
- 5: Comprehensive accessibility, WCAG AA compliance, inclusive design throughout
- 4: Strong accessibility features, good compliance, inclusive approach
- 3: Basic accessibility addressed, some compliance
- 2: Minimal accessibility consideration
- 1: Accessibility largely ignored

**Clarity and Organization (4 points)**
- 4: Excellent structure, clear storytelling, perfect timing
- 3: Good organization, clear flow, appropriate timing
- 2: Adequate structure, some clarity issues, timing slightly off
- 1: Poor organization, unclear flow, significant timing issues

**Demonstration (3 points)**
- 3: Excellent demo, all features shown clearly, smooth execution
- 2: Good demo, most features shown, minor technical issues
- 1: Basic demo, limited features shown, or technical problems

**Reflection and Insights (3 points)**
- 3: Thoughtful reflection, clear lessons learned, strong HCI connection
- 2: Good reflection, some insights, basic HCI connection
- 1: Minimal reflection, limited insights

   - What accessibility testing did you conduct?

   - What feedback led to changes?
   - What would you do differently?
