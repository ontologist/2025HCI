## Slide-by-Slide Speaking Points | スライド別スピーキングポイント

### Slide 1: From Research to Design Artifacts | 調査からデザイン成果物へ

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Connect to previous week** | 前週とつなげる: "Last week you conducted user research. Today we transform those insights into design tools."
- **Check understanding** | 理解をチェック: "Who can share one key insight from your interviews?" (Get 2-3 responses)
- **Set expectations** | 期待を設定: "By end of class, you'll have personas and journey maps ready to guide your designs"

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Research must inform design** | 調査がデザインを通知する必要がある: Don't skip this connection
- **Artifacts are communication tools** | 成果物はコミュニケーションツール: Share insights with teams
- **Everything connects** | すべてが接続: Personas → Journeys → Scenarios → Requirements

#### Common Questions | よくある質問
- **"How many personas should I create?"** | 「いくつのペルソナを作成すべきですか？」
  - **Answer:** 2-4 primary personas. Enough to represent diversity, not so many you lose focus.
- **"What if my research only found 2 users?"** | 「調査が2人のユーザーしか見つけなかった場合はどうしますか？」
  - **Answer:** Personas represent patterns across users, not individuals. Look for behavioral patterns.

### Slide 2: What Are Personas? | ペルソナとは？

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Define clearly** | 明確に定義: "Personas are fictional but realistic representations based on real research data"
- **Show examples** | 例を表示: Display a well-crafted persona (prepared in advance)
- **Emphasize data foundation** | データ基盤を強調: "Every detail comes from research, not imagination"

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Personas create empathy** | ペルソナが共感を作る: Users become real, memorable people
- **Focus design decisions** | デザイン決定に焦点: "Would Sarah use this?" is a powerful question
- **Communication tool** | コミュニケーションツール: Share research findings efficiently

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Contrast good and bad** | 良いものと悪いものを対比: "This is a marketing profile vs. a design persona"
- **Use student example** | 学生の例を使用: "Think about designing for your classmate - specific, not generic"
- **Connect to projects** | プロジェクトとつなげる: "Your final project needs at least one solid persona"

#### Common Misconceptions | 一般的な誤解
- **Personas are not stereotypes** | ペルソナはステレオタイプではない: Based on behavior, not demographics alone
- **Personas are not fictional stories** | ペルソナは架空の物語ではない: Every element traces to research data
- **Personas are not marketing profiles** | ペルソナはマーケティングプロファイルではない: Design tool, not sales tool

### Slide 3: Anatomy of a Persona | ペルソナの構造

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Walk through each component** | 各コンポーネントを歩く: Show how each piece contributes to understanding
- **Show complete example** | 完全な例を表示: Full persona with all components filled in
- **Explain relationships** | 関係を説明: How goals connect to pain points, etc.

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Every component has purpose** | すべてのコンポーネントに目的がある: Nothing is decorative
- **Quote is authentic** | 引用は本物: Direct from research interviews
- **Needs link to research** | ニーズは調査にリンク: From Week 3 user needs statements

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Use analogy** | 類推を使用: "Like a character profile, but for real users"
- **Show progression** | 進行を示す: "Start with basics, add depth from research"
- **Emphasize evidence** | 証拠を強調: "Note how each section references interview data"

#### Interactive Activity | インタラクティブアクティビティ
- Display incomplete persona, ask students what's missing
- Have students identify which elements come from research vs. assumption
- Practice writing one component together (e.g., goals based on sample research)

### Slide 4: Creating Data-Driven Personas | データ駆動のペルソナの作成

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Demonstrate process** | プロセスを実演: Walk through from research to persona
- **Show segmentation** | セグメンテーションを表示: How to identify user groups
- **Explain validation** | 検証を説明: Every persona element must trace to data

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Start with research review** | 調査レビューから始める: Don't create personas from memory
- **Look for patterns** | パターンを探す: Similar goals, behaviors, pain points
- **2-4 personas is optimal** | 2-4個のペルソナが最適: Enough diversity, manageable focus

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Use student research** | 学生の調査を使用: "Look at your Week 3 affinity mapping - what groups emerged?"
- **Show segmentation axes** | セグメンテーション軸を表示: Experience level, frequency, goals, context
- **Practice clustering** | クラスタリングを練習: "These 3 users all prioritize speed - that's one segment"

#### Demonstration | 実演
- Use anonymized student research from previous year
- Show how 5 interviews become 2 distinct personas
- Highlight how behavioral patterns (not demographics) drive segmentation

### Slide 5: Persona Example | ペルソナの例

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Present complete persona** | 完全なペルソナを提示: Sarah Chen example
- **Trace to research** | 調査に遡る: "Notice evidence: '4/5 users mentioned work schedule conflicts'"
- **Show how it guides design** | デザインをどのようにガイドするかを示す: "This tells us mobile optimization is critical"

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Realistic and specific** | 現実的で具体的: Not "student" but "21-year-old business student working part-time"
- **Quote captures voice** | 引用が声をキャプチャ: Hear the user's perspective
- **Pain points drive design** | ペインポイントがデザインを推進: These become opportunities

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Read it out loud** | 声に出して読む: Makes persona feel like a real person
- **Ask design questions** | デザイン質問をする: "What would Sarah need in a mobile interface?"
- **Compare to research** | 調査と比較: "See how this connects to interview findings?"

#### Interactive Exercise | インタラクティブな演習
- "If you were designing for Sarah, would you prioritize desktop or mobile? Why?"
- "What's Sarah's biggest frustration? How might we address it?"
- Have students identify which elements came from which research methods

### Slide 6: User Journey Maps | ユーザージャーニーマップ

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Define journey maps** | ジャーニーマップを定義: Visual representation of experience over time
- **Explain components** | コンポーネントを説明: Stages, actions, touchpoints, emotions
- **Show why they matter** | なぜ重要かを示す: Identify pain points and opportunities

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Time-based view** | 時間ベースのビュー: Shows complete experience, not just one moment
- **Emotion is critical** | 感情は重要: How users feel matters as much as what they do
- **Reveals opportunities** | 機会を明らかにする: Pain points become design interventions

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Use metaphor** | メタファーを使用: "Like a story timeline with emotional arc"
- **Connect to personas** | ペルソナとつなげる: "Journey maps bring personas to life"
- **Emphasize research basis** | 調査基盤を強調: "Comes from observing real users"

#### Visual Support | ビジュアルサポート
- Show journey map examples on screen
- If possible, display physical journey map on wall
- Use color coding to highlight emotions (green = positive, red = frustration)

### Slide 7: Creating User Journey Maps | ユーザージャーニーマップの作成

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Walk through 7-step process** | 7ステップのプロセスを歩く: From persona selection to opportunity identification
- **Demonstrate each step** | 各ステップを実演: Use Sarah's registration journey as example
- **Emphasize iteration** | 反復を強調: First draft won't be perfect - refine as you go

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Start with persona and scenario** | ペルソナとシナリオから始める: Clear scope before mapping
- **4-6 stages is typical** | 4-6段階が典型的: Enough detail, not overwhelming
- **Emotions reveal pain points** | 感情がペインポイントを明らかにする: Where frustration peaks = where to improve

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Use timeline analogy** | タイムライン類推を使用: "Think of it as a movie storyboard"
- **Practice stage naming** | ステージ命名を練習: "Use verbs: Awareness, Research, Decision"
- **Show emotional curve** | 感情曲線を表示: Graph emotional highs and lows

#### Common Student Mistakes | 一般的な学生の間違い
- **Too many stages** | 段階が多すぎる: More than 8 becomes unwieldy
- **Missing emotions** | 感情を見逃す: Journey map without emotion is just a process diagram
- **Not research-based** | 調査ベースではない: Must connect to actual observations

### Slide 8: Journey Map Example | ジャーニーマップの例

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Present Sarah's journey** | サラのジャーニーを提示: Walk through all 6 stages
- **Highlight emotional shifts** | 感情的変化をハイライト: Point out where frustration builds
- **Identify opportunities** | 機会を特定: "See how pain points become design opportunities?"

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Complete experience** | 完全な体験: From awareness to confirmation
- **Pain point clustering** | ペインポイントのクラスタリング: Stage 4-5 have multiple frustrations
- **Opportunities list** | 機会リスト: Five concrete design interventions identified

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Read it like a story** | 物語のように読む: "Sarah starts motivated, becomes frustrated, ends disappointed"
- **Point out evidence** | 証拠を指摘: "Notice touchpoints - mobile then laptop - from research observation"
- **Connect to design** | デザインとつなげる: "Visual schedule builder directly addresses pain point"

#### Interactive Discussion | インタラクティブディスカッション
- "Where is Sarah most frustrated? What could we do to help?"
- "What surprised you about this journey?"
- "Which opportunity would you prioritize first? Why?"

### Slide 9: Scenarios | シナリオ

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Define scenarios** | シナリオを定義: Narrative descriptions of persona using design
- **Explain three types** | 3つのタイプを説明: Current state, future state, edge case
- **Show how scenarios guide design** | シナリオがデザインをどのようにガイドするかを示す: Make abstract concrete

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Scenarios tell stories** | シナリオが物語を語る: Narrative format, not bullet points
- **Context matters** | コンテキストが重要: Where, when, why user is doing this
- **Test designs mentally** | 精神的にデザインをテスト: Walk through scenario to validate solution

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Use movie analogy** | 映画の類推を使用: "Like a screenplay for your design"
- **Contrast types** | タイプを対比: Current state shows problems, future state shows solutions
- **Emphasize specificity** | 特異性を強調: "Not 'user accesses system' but 'Sarah opens app on iPhone during lunch'"

---

### Slide 10: Writing Effective Scenarios | 効果的なシナリオの書き方

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Present 6-part structure** | 6部構成を提示: Title, persona, context, goal, actions, outcome
- **Show good vs. bad examples** | 良い例 vs 悪い例を示す: Generic vs. specific scenarios
- **Practice together** | 一緒に練習: Write scenario opening as a class

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Be specific** | 具体的である: Names, devices, times, places
- **Include context** | コンテキストを含める: Environmental factors matter
- **Show interactions** | 相互作用を示す: User action → system response

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Use template** | テンプレートを使用: Provide structure for consistency
- **Read examples aloud** | 例を声に出して読む: Hear the difference between good and bad
- **Practice specificity** | 特異性を練習: Transform vague to specific together

#### Interactive Exercise | インタラクティブな演習
- Give generic scenario: "User registers for courses"
- Have students make it specific: Add who, where, when, device, context
- Compare results - see how specificity brings it to life

### Slide 11: Scenario Example | シナリオの例

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Present current state scenario** | 現在の状態シナリオを提示: Sarah's frustrating experience
- **Contrast with future state** | 将来の状態と対比: Same goal, improved experience
- **Highlight differences** | 違いをハイライト: Time, steps, emotions, outcome

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Current state shows problems** | 現在の状態が問題を示す: 35 minutes, frustrated, third choices
- **Future state shows solution** | 将来の状態がソリューションを示す: 12 minutes, confident, first choices
- **Specific interactions** | 具体的な相互作用: "Taps 'Search Courses' and sees..."

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Read dramatically** | 劇的に読む: Emphasize emotional differences
- **Count improvements** | 改善を数える: Time savings, better outcome, positive emotions
- **Extract requirements** | 要件を抽出: "This scenario tells us we need conflict detection"

#### Discussion Questions | ディスカッション質問
- "What specific features made the future scenario better?"
- "How did Sarah's emotions change?"
- "What can we learn about mobile design from this?"

### Slide 12: From Artifacts to Requirements | 成果物から要件へ

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション

## Activity Transition | アクティビティ移行

### Before Starting Activity | アクティビティ開始前
1. **Verify bot access** | ボットアクセスを確認: All students logged in to persona module
2. **Distribute materials** | 教材を配布: Sticky notes, markers, large paper for journey maps
3. **Ensure research access** | 調査アクセスを確保: Students can reference Week 3 findings
4. **Quick demo** | クイックデモ: Show bot interface for persona creation

### During Activity | アクティビティ中
1. **Circulate actively** | 積極的に巡回: Monitor all students
2. **Answer questions** | 質問に答える: But encourage bot use first
3. **Watch for issues** | 問題を監視: Common problems detailed below
4. **Manage time** | 時間を管理: Give warnings at transitions

### Common Issues During Activity | アクティビティ中の一般的な問題

#### Issue 1: Students Create Fictional Personas | 問題1：学生が架空のペルソナを作成
- **Signs:** Details not traceable to research, too creative/imaginative
- **Intervention:** "Show me where in your research this came from"
- **Prevention:** Emphasize evidence-based approach in lecture

#### Issue 2: Too Many or Too Few Personas | 問題2：ペルソナが多すぎるまたは少なすぎる
- **Signs:** 6+ personas (too many) or only 1 persona (too few)
- **Intervention:** "What are the main behavioral patterns? Group similar users"
- **Bot Help:** Bot suggests optimal number based on segmentation

#### Issue 3: Generic, Vague Personas | 問題3：一般的で曖昧なペルソナ
- **Signs:** "College student who likes apps" - too broad
- **Intervention:** "Add specific details: What's their exact situation? Goals? Pain points?"
- **Reminder:** Use quote from actual research to add authenticity

#### Issue 4: Journey Maps Without Emotions | 問題4：感情のないジャーニーマップ
- **Signs:** Only listing actions and touchpoints, no emotional data
- **Intervention:** "How did the user FEEL at each stage? Check your interview notes"
- **Bot Help:** Bot prompts for emotional data at each stage

#### Issue 5: Skipping Opportunities | 問題5：機会をスキップ
- **Signs:** Journey map shows pain points but no design opportunities
- **Intervention:** "Each pain point is a design opportunity - what could we do here?"
- **Reminder:** "This is why we map journeys - to find where to improve"

#### Issue 6: Scenarios Too Abstract | 問題6：シナリオが抽象的すぎる
- **Signs:** "User accesses system and completes task" - no detail
- **Intervention:** "Who? Where? What device? What time? What's the context?"
- **Bot Help:** Bot provides scenario template with prompts for specificity

#### Issue 7: Requirements Not Linked to Artifacts | 問題7：成果物にリンクされていない要件
- **Signs:** Requirements list that doesn't connect to personas or journeys
- **Intervention:** "Show me which pain point this addresses"
- **Reminder:** "Every requirement should trace back to research evidence"

## Post-Activity Discussion | アクティビティ後のディスカッション

## Assessment Notes | 評価ノート

### What to Look For in Submissions | 提出物で何を見るか

#### Persona Quality | ペルソナの質
- **Complete components** | 完全なコンポーネント: All 6 essential sections included
- **Evidence-based** | 証拠ベース: Details trace to research findings
- **Specific and realistic** | 具体的で現実的: Not generic, includes authentic details
- **Authentic quote** | 本物の引用: Direct from research interviews
- **Clear needs** | 明確なニーズ: Prioritized list from research

#### Journey Map Quality | ジャーニーマップの質
- **Clear stages** | 明確な段階: 4-6 distinct phases with descriptive names
- **Detailed actions** | 詳細なアクション: Specific behaviors at each stage
- **Touchpoints identified** | タッチポイント識別: Where user interacts with system
- **Emotional data** | 感情データ: Feelings captured at each stage
- **Pain points highlighted** | ペインポイントハイライト: Problems clearly marked
- **Opportunities listed** | 機会リスト: Design interventions identified

#### Scenario Quality | シナリオの質
- **Complete structure** | 完全な構造: Title, persona, context, goal, actions, outcome
- **Specificity** | 特異性: Detailed context, devices, timing
- **Current and future states** | 現在と将来の状態: Both scenarios written
- **Realistic interactions** | 現実的な相互作用: Step-by-step actions
- **Emotional elements** | 感情要素: How user feels throughout

#### Requirements Quality | 要件の質
- **Linked to artifacts** | 成果物にリンク: Each requirement traces to persona/journey/scenario
- **Prioritized** | 優先順位付け: P0, P1, P2 classification with rationale
- **Specific and actionable** | 具体的で実行可能: Clear enough to guide design
- **Evidence-based** | 証拠ベース: Connected to research findings

#### Bot Interaction Evidence | ボットインタラクション証拠
- **Screenshots** | スクリーンショット: Key bot conversations
- **Template usage** | テンプレート使用: Bot-provided frameworks utilized
- **Guidance followed** | ガイダンスに従う: Evidence of bot suggestions applied
- **Iteration** | 反復: Refinements based on bot feedback

### Grading Rubric | 評価基準

#### Excellent (90-100%) | 優秀（90-100％）
- 2-4 comprehensive personas with all components, evidence-based
- Detailed journey map with 5-6 stages, emotions, and opportunities
- Both current and future state scenarios, highly specific
- 8-12 prioritized requirements with clear traceability
- Thoughtful reflection on insights gained
- Extensive bot interaction evidence

#### Good (80-89%) | 良い（80-89％）
- 1-3 solid personas with most components, mostly evidence-based
- Journey map with 4-5 stages, some emotional data
- Both scenarios present, reasonably specific
- 6-10 requirements with some prioritization
- Adequate reflection
- Good bot interaction evidence

#### Satisfactory (70-79%) | 満足（70-79％）
- 1-2 basic personas with key components
- Journey map with 3-4 stages, limited emotional data
- At least one scenario, some specificity
- 4-6 requirements, basic prioritization
- Basic reflection
- Some bot interaction evidence

#### Needs Improvement (<70%) | 改善が必要（<70％）
- Incomplete or generic personas
- Limited journey map, missing components
- Vague or missing scenarios
- Few requirements, no clear prioritization
- Minimal reflection
- Little bot interaction evidence

## Troubleshooting Guide | トラブルシューティングガイド

### Technical Issues | 技術的問題
- **Bot not loading persona module** | ボットがペルソナモジュールを読み込まない: Clear cache, refresh, try different browser
- **Can't access Week 3 research** | 週3の調査にアクセスできない: Verify file locations, have backup copies
- **Journey mapping tool issues** | ジャーニーマッピングツールの問題: Switch to paper-based alternative

### Process Issues | プロセスの問題
- **Students can't identify segments** | 学生がセグメントを特定できない: Guide affinity mapping review, look for behavioral patterns
- **Personas too similar** | ペルソナが似すぎている: Emphasize different goals, behaviors, contexts
- **Journey maps too high-level** | ジャーニーマップが高レベルすぎる: Add specific actions, emotions, quotes from research

### Content Issues | コンテンツの問題
- **Fictional details in personas** | ペルソナの架空の詳細: Require evidence citation for each element
- **Missing emotional data** | 感情データの欠如: Prompt to review interview notes for feelings
- **Requirements don't match artifacts** | 要件が成果物と一致しない: Show traceability requirement, have them connect dots
