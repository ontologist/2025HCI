### Slide 3: Types of User Research | ユーザー調査のタイプ

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Draw matrix on board** | ボードにマトリックスを描く: Qualitative vs. Quantitative, Attitudinal vs. Behavioral
- **Use examples** | 例を使用: "If I want to know WHY students struggle, I interview. If I want to know HOW MANY, I survey."
- **Emphasize difference** | 違いを強調: "What people say vs. what people do - this is crucial!"

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Right tool for right question** | 正しい質問のための正しいツール: Different methods answer different questions
- **Early research is qualitative** | 初期調査は質的: Understand before measuring
- **Behavior > Attitudes** | 行動 > 態度: Watch what they do, not just what they say

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Ask for examples** | 例を求める: "When would you use a survey vs. an interview?"
- **Address confusion** | 混乱に対処: Students often confuse qualitative with quantitative
- **Provide decision framework** | 決定フレームワークを提供: "Start with 'what do I want to learn?'"

### Slide 5: Conducting Effective Interviews | 効果的なインタビューの実施

### Slide 6: The Art of Asking Questions | 質問の技術

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Show bad vs. good questions** | 悪い質問 vs 良い質問を表示: Use concrete examples
- **Practice transformation** | 変換を練習: Turn closed questions into open ones
- **Demonstrate probing** | 探索を実演: Show how "why" leads to insights

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Open-ended is gold** | オープンエンドは金: Gets stories, not yes/no
- **Behavior beats hypotheticals** | 行動が仮定に勝る: Past actions are reliable
- **Probing reveals depth** | 探索が深さを明らかにする: Follow up on interesting answers

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Interactive exercise** | インタラクティブな演習: "Let's transform this question together"
- **Use student examples** | 学生の例を使用: "What's a closed question about course registration?"
- **Practice probing** | 探索を練習: "Ask me about my morning - practice 'why' and 'tell me more'"

#### Common Student Mistakes | 一般的な学生の間違い
- **Leading questions** | 誘導質問: "Don't you think..." suggests the answer
- **Double-barreled questions** | 二重質問: Asking two things at once confuses
- **Too many closed questions** | クローズド質問が多すぎる: Results in surface-level data

### Slide 7: Active Listening Skills | アクティブリスニングスキル

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Demonstrate listening** | リスニングを実演: Have student speak, show active listening techniques
- **Explain silence** | 沈黙を説明: "Count to 5 in your head - don't fill every gap"
- **Show body language** | ボディランゲージを示す: What to watch for in participants

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Listening is active work** | リスニングはアクティブな作業: Not passive hearing
- **Silence is powerful** | 沈黙は強力: Let them think
- **Body language reveals emotion** | ボディランゲージが感情を明らかにする: Watch for frustration, excitement

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Practice silence** | 沈黙を練習: "Let's sit in silence for 5 seconds - feels long, right?"
- **Show note-taking** | メモ取りを表示: "Capture quotes, behaviors, emotions"
- **Emphasize presence** | 存在を強調: "Put your phone away - be fully there"

#### Interactive Activity | インタラクティブアクティビティ
- Ask a student a question, demonstrate active listening techniques
- Point out: nodding, eye contact, paraphrasing, not interrupting
- Have students pair up for 2-minute practice

### Slide 8: Observation Methods | 観察方法

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Explain types** | タイプを説明: Direct observation, contextual inquiry, think-aloud
- **Show differences** | 違いを表示: When to use each method
- **Emphasize "do vs. say"** | 「する vs 言う」を強調: Behavior is truth

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Observation complements interviews** | 観察がインタビューを補完: Use both together
- **Context matters** | コンテキストが重要: Where/when affects behavior

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Show video example** | ビデオ例を表示: If available, show observation in action
- **Practice observation** | 観察を練習: "Watch me use this app for 30 seconds - what do you notice?"

### Slide 9: Surveys: When and How to Use Them | 調査：いつどのように使用するか

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Clarify purpose** | 目的を明確にする: "Surveys quantify, interviews explore"
- **Show survey examples** | 調査例を表示: Good and bad survey questions
- **Explain limitations** | 制限を説明: "Can't discover unknown problems with surveys"

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Use after interviews** | インタビュー後に使用: Validate patterns found qualitatively
- **Keep short** | 短く保つ: 5-10 minutes maximum
- **Clear language** | 明確な言語: No jargon, one question at a time

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Show bad examples** | 悪い例を表示: Leading, ambiguous, double-barreled questions
- **Explain when NOT to survey** | 調査しない時を説明: Early exploration, understanding "why"
- **Connect to projects** | プロジェクトとつなげる: "You might use surveys later to validate designs"

### Slide 10: Analyzing Qualitative Data | 質的データの分析

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Explain affinity mapping** | アフィニティマッピングを説明: Visual, collaborative analysis method
- **Walk through process** | プロセスを歩く: Extract, group, label, prioritize
- **Show example** | 例を表示: Photos of affinity mapping in action

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Analysis is systematic** | 分析は体系的: Not just gut feeling
- **Patterns emerge** | パターンが現れる: Look for frequency and intensity
- **Physical/digital both work** | 物理/デジタル両方が機能: Sticky notes or Miro/Mural

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Make it concrete** | 具体的にする: "Each quote or finding on one note"
- **Emphasize grouping** | グループ化を強調: "Let patterns emerge naturally"
- **Show messy process** | 混乱したプロセスを表示: "It looks chaotic at first - that's normal!"

#### Demonstration | 実演
- If possible, show quick affinity mapping with student input
- Use whiteboard or projected digital tool
- Demonstrate moving notes around to find patterns

### Slide 11: Synthesizing User Needs | ユーザーニーズの統合

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Introduce framework** | フレームワークを紹介: User needs statement template
- **Show transformation** | 変換を表示: From raw data to actionable needs
- **Practice together** | 一緒に練習: Create one need statement as a class

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **User-focused language** | ユーザー中心の言語: About people, not systems
- **Specific and actionable** | 具体的で実行可能: Guide design decisions
- **Evidence-based** | 証拠ベース: Link to research data

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Use template** | テンプレートを使用: "[User] needs [goal] because [insight] but currently [problem]"
- **Show bad examples** | 悪い例を表示: Solution-focused vs. problem-focused
- **Practice writing** | 書くことを練習: Have students draft one on the spot

#### Interactive Exercise | インタラクティブな演習
- Present research finding: "3 students said registration is too slow"
- Work together to write user need statement
- Compare to solution-focused alternative to show difference

## Activity Transition | アクティビティ移行

### Before Starting Activity | アクティビティ開始前
1. **Verify bot access** | ボットアクセスを確認: All students logged in
2. **Form pairs** | ペアを形成: For interview practice
3. **Distribute materials** | 教材を配布: Note-taking sheets, templates
4. **Quick demo** | クイックデモ: Show bot interface for research module

### During Activity | アクティビティ中
1. **Circulate actively** | 積極的に巡回: Monitor all groups
2. **Answer questions** | 質問に答える: But encourage bot use first
3. **Watch for issues** | 問題を監視: Intervene if students stuck
4. **Manage time** | 時間を管理: Give warnings at transitions

### Common Issues During Activity | アクティビティ中の一般的な問題

#### Issue 1: Students Skip Interview Guide Creation | 問題1：学生がインタビューガイド作成をスキップ
- **Signs:** Jumping straight to interviews without preparation
- **Intervention:** "Stop - you need a guide first. Use the bot's template."
- **Prevention:** Emphasize importance in lecture

#### Issue 2: Closed Questions in Interview Guide | 問題2：インタビューガイドのクローズド質問
- **Signs:** Guide full of yes/no questions
- **Intervention:** "Let's transform these. How can we make this open-ended?"
- **Bot Help:** Bot provides question transformation suggestions

#### Issue 3: Not Taking Detailed Notes | 問題3：詳細なメモを取らない
- **Signs:** Sparse notes, no quotes
- **Intervention:** "You'll need direct quotes for analysis. Write them down."
- **Reminder:** "If recording, still take notes for key moments"

#### Issue 4: Helping During Observation | 問題4：観察中に助ける
- **Signs:** Interviewer jumping in to help participant
- **Intervention:** "Let them struggle - that's valuable data!"
- **Reminder:** "Observation is about watching, not helping"

#### Issue 5: Surface-Level Analysis | 問題5：表面レベルの分析
- **Signs:** Not finding patterns, listing individual quotes
- **Intervention:** "Look for themes. What do multiple participants say?"
- **Bot Help:** Bot provides affinity mapping structure

#### Issue 6: Solution-Focused User Needs | 問題6：ソリューション中心のユーザーニーズ
- **Signs:** "Users need a button to..." instead of "Users need to quickly..."
- **Intervention:** "Describe the need, not the solution. What are they trying to do?"
- **Bot Help:** Bot validates user needs statements

## Post-Activity Discussion | アクティビティ後のディスカッション

### Reflection Questions | 振り返り質問
1. **"What surprised you during your interviews?"** | 「インタビュー中に何が驚きましたか？」
2. **"What was challenging about asking open-ended questions?"** | 「オープンエンド質問をすることの何が挑戦的でしたか？」
3. **"Did users say one thing but do another?"** | 「ユーザーは一つのことを言って別のことをしましたか？」
4. **"What patterns did you find in your data?"** | 「データでどんなパターンを見つけましたか？」
5. **"How did the bot help you through the process?"** | 「ボットはプロセスをどのように支援しましたか？」

### Key Learnings to Reinforce | 強化すべき主要な学び
- **Open questions are harder but better** | オープン質問は難しいが良い: Takes practice
- **Silence is uncomfortable but valuable** | 沈黙は不快だが価値がある: Wait for insights
- **Analysis reveals surprises** | 分析が驚きを明らかにする: Patterns you didn't expect
- **User needs guide design** | ユーザーニーズがデザインをガイド: Foundation for next steps

### Common Student Insights | 一般的な学生の洞察
- "I realized I talk too much in conversations normally"
- "Users couldn't articulate what they wanted until I probed"
- "What people said they do wasn't what they actually did"
- "Patterns emerged that I never would have guessed"

## Assessment Notes | 評価ノート

### What to Look For in Submissions | 提出物で何を見るか

#### Interview Guide Quality | インタビューガイドの質
- **Open-ended questions** | オープンエンド質問: Majority should be open
- **Logical flow** | 論理的流れ: Warm-up to deep questions
- **Probing prompts** | 探索プロンプト: Follow-up question ideas
- **Appropriate scope** | 適切な範囲: 10-15 main questions

#### Interview Execution Evidence | インタビュー実行証拠
- **Detailed notes** | 詳細なメモ: Rich, specific information
- **Direct quotes** | 直接の引用: Participant words in quotation marks
- **Behavioral observations** | 行動観察: What they did, not just said
- **Emotional cues noted** | 感情的手がかりをメモ: Frustration, excitement markers

#### Analysis Quality | 分析の質
- **Affinity mapping** | アフィニティマッピング: Clear groupings and themes
- **Pattern identification** | パターン識別: Frequency and intensity noted
- **Evidence-based** | 証拠ベース: Linked to specific data
- **Prioritization** | 優先順位付け: Most important themes identified

#### User Needs Statements | ユーザーニーズステートメント
- **User-focused** | ユーザー中心: About people, not systems
- **Specific** | 具体的: Clear and actionable
- **Evidence-based** | 証拠ベース: Linked to research findings
- **Problem-oriented** | 問題指向: Not solution-focused

#### Bot Interaction Evidence | ボットインタラクション証拠
- **Screenshots** | スクリーンショット: Key bot conversations
- **Template usage** | テンプレート使用: Bot-provided frameworks
- **Guidance followed** | ガイダンスに従う: Evidence of bot suggestions applied
- **Reflection** | 振り返り: How bot helped

### Grading Rubric | 評価基準

#### Excellent (90-100%) | 優秀（90-100％）
- Comprehensive interview guide with all open-ended questions
- Rich, detailed interview notes with multiple direct quotes
- Clear affinity mapping with well-defined themes
- 5-7 specific, evidence-based user needs statements
- Thoughtful reflection on insights and process
- Extensive bot interaction evidence

#### Good (80-89%) | 良い（80-89％）
- Solid interview guide with mostly open-ended questions
- Good interview notes with some direct quotes
- Affinity mapping present with identifiable themes
- 3-5 user needs statements, mostly specific
- Adequate reflection on process
- Good bot interaction evidence

#### Satisfactory (70-79%) | 満足（70-79％）
- Basic interview guide with some closed questions
- Interview notes present but limited detail
- Basic analysis with some pattern identification
- 2-3 user needs statements, some vague
- Basic reflection
- Some bot interaction evidence

#### Needs Improvement (<70%) | 改善が必要（<70％）
- Incomplete interview guide or mostly closed questions
- Sparse interview notes without quotes
- Limited or missing analysis
- Few or poorly formed user needs statements
- Minimal reflection
- Little bot interaction evidence

## Emergency Backup Plans | 緊急バックアッププラン

### If Bot Portal Fails | ボットポータルが失敗した場合
1. Use provided paper templates for all activities
2. Instructor provides verbal guidance for each stage
3. Students work in small groups for peer support
4. Extend activity time to compensate for lack of bot efficiency

### If Students Can't Record | 学生が録音できない場合
1. Emphasize detailed note-taking techniques
2. Suggest pairing - one interviewer, one note-taker
3. Immediately transcribe key quotes after interview
4. Use memory aids and reconstruction

### If Time Runs Short | 時間が不足した場合
1. **Priority:** Complete interviews and basic analysis
2. **Skip if needed:** Detailed affinity mapping (assign as homework)
3. **Simplify:** Create 3 user needs instead of 5-7
4. **Extend:** Make report completion homework
