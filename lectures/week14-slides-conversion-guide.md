## Timeline and Time Management:
## タイムラインと時間管理：

### Class Session Breakdown (100 minutes):

**Introduction (10 minutes):**
- Slide 1-2: Welcome and agenda
- Set celebratory tone
- Explain format
- Announce order

**Presentations (60-75 minutes):**
- Slides 3-16: Background slides as needed
- 4-6 student presentations
- 12-15 minutes each (7-10 min + 2-3 min Q&A + 2 min feedback)
- 5-minute breaks after 3-4 presentations

**Wrap-up (10-15 minutes):**
- Slides 12-15: Course reflection and celebration
- Collective achievements
- Thank you messages
- Bot assessment explanation
- Final celebration

### Time Management Tools:

**Visual Timer:**
- Large countdown timer
- Color-coded warnings (green > yellow > red)
- Visible to presenter and audience
- 2-minute and 1-minute warnings

**Presentation Schedule:**
- Display current presenter
- Show next 2-3 presenters
- Indicate break times
- Track progress through schedule

## Bot Assessment Coordination:
## ボット評価調整：

### Explaining the Bot Assessment:

**Key Information to Present:**
- Purpose: Test understanding of HCI principles and design rationale
- Format: Conversational interview
- Duration: 15-20 minutes per student
- Topics: User research, design decisions, usability, accessibility, iterations
- Access: Bot portal
- Schedule: After presentations or separately
- Grading: 15% of final grade

**Scheduling Options:**
1. Immediately after all presentations (if time permits)
2. Separate session later in week
3. Individually scheduled appointments
4. Flexible completion window

**Student Preparation:**
- Review project and design decisions
- Prepare to justify choices
- Understand HCI principles applied
- Ready to discuss iterations and testing
- Can reference materials but no AI assistance

## Emergency Backup Plans:
## 緊急バックアッププラン：

### Technical Failure Scenarios:

**Projection System Fails:**
- Move to different classroom
- Use personal devices to show prototypes
- Allow presentation without visual slides
- Focus on demo and discussion

**Internet Connectivity Issues:**
- Use mobile hotspot
- Switch to offline prototypes
- Show screenshots instead of live demos
- Continue with non-demo portions

**Student Device Problems:**
- Use instructor's computer
- Present from backup files
- Show on personal device
- Reschedule if necessary

**Student Absence:**
- Contact immediately
- Offer makeup presentation
- Accept video submission
- Schedule alternative time
