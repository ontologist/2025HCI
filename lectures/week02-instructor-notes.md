#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション

### Slide 3: The Design Thinking Process | デザイン思考プロセス

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Walk through each stage** | 各段階を歩く: "Let me show you what each stage looks like in practice"
- **Use analogies** | 類推を使用: "Think of it like cooking - you don't start by putting food in the oven"
- **Emphasize the flow** | 流れを強調: "Each stage builds on the previous one"

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Empathize is crucial** | 共感が重要: Without understanding users, everything else fails
- **Define focuses the problem** | 定義は問題に焦点: Clear problem statements lead to better solutions
- **Ideate is about quantity** | 発想は量について: Generate many ideas, evaluate later
- **Prototype is about learning** | プロトタイプは学習について: Build to think, not to impress
- **Test validates assumptions** | テストは仮定を検証: Real users tell you what works

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Use hand gestures** | 手のジェスチャーを使用: Point to each stage as you explain
- **Ask for examples** | 例を求める: "Can anyone think of a time they solved a problem by understanding someone else's needs?"
- **Address concerns** | 懸念に対処: "This might seem like a lot, but we'll practice each stage today"

### Slide 4: Stage 1: Empathize - Understanding Users | 段階1：共感 - ユーザーの理解

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Demonstrate interview techniques** | インタビュー技法を実演: "Let me show you how to ask good questions"
- **Role-play an interview** | インタビューをロールプレイ: Have a student volunteer for a quick demo
- **Show empathy mapping** | 共感マッピングを表示: Draw a simple empathy map on the board

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Listen for emotions** | 感情に耳を傾ける: "It's frustrating" tells you more than "I want a button"
- **Ask "why" multiple times** | 「なぜ」を複数回尋ねる: Dig deeper into surface-level answers
- **Observe behavior** | 行動を観察: What people do vs. what they say they do

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Practice with students** | 学生と練習: "Let's try a quick interview right now"
- **Show bad examples** | 悪い例を表示: "Here's what NOT to ask: 'Do you like this app?'"
- **Emphasize empathy** | 共感を強調: "You're not just collecting data - you're trying to understand another person"

---

### Slide 5: Stage 2: Define - Articulating the Problem | 段階2：定義 - 問題の明確化

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Write POV statements on board** | ボードにPOV文を書く: Show the format clearly
- **Practice with examples** | 例で練習: "Let's write a POV statement for a common problem"
- **Show transformation** | 変換を表示: Turn a bad problem statement into a good one

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **User-focused language** | ユーザー中心の言語: "Students need..." not "The app should..."
- **Specific and actionable** | 具体的で実行可能: Vague problems lead to vague solutions
- **Based on real insights** | 実際の洞察に基づく: Not assumptions or guesses

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Use student examples** | 学生の例を使用: "Let's write a POV for something you all experience"
- **Show common mistakes** | 一般的な間違いを表示: "Here's what students often write..."
- **Practice HMW questions** | HMW質問を練習: "Turn this problem into a 'How might we' question"

---

### Slide 6: Stage 3: Ideate - Generating Solutions | 段階3：発想 - ソリューションの生成

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Demonstrate rapid sketching** | 迅速なスケッチを実演: Draw 3-4 quick interface ideas
- **Show brainstorming rules** | ブレインストーミングルールを表示: Write them on the board
- **Practice "worst possible idea"** | 「最悪の可能なアイデア」を練習: "Let's design the worst course registration system"

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Quantity over quality** | 質より量: Generate many ideas first, evaluate later
- **Defer judgment** | 判断を延期: No criticism during brainstorming
- **Build on ideas** | アイデアを構築: "Yes, and..." instead of "Yes, but..."

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Set time limits** | 時間制限を設定: "You have 2 minutes to sketch 8 ideas"
- **Encourage wild ideas** | ワイルドなアイデアを奨励: "The crazier, the better - we can refine later"
- **Show evaluation criteria** | 評価基準を表示: "How do we know which ideas are good?"

---

### Slide 7: Stage 4: Prototype - Building to Think | 段階4：試作 - 考えるために構築

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Show fidelity examples** | 忠実度の例を表示: Bring examples of low, mid, high fidelity prototypes
- **Demonstrate paper prototyping** | 紙のプロトタイピングを実演: Draw a quick interface on paper
- **Emphasize speed** | 速度を強調: "This should take 10-15 minutes, not hours"

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Low-fidelity is perfect** | 低忠実度が完璧: Focus on concepts, not visual details
- **Fail fast** | 早く失敗: Learn quickly from mistakes
- **Test the interaction** | インタラクションをテスト: Not the visual design

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Show examples** | 例を表示: Bring paper prototypes from previous classes
- **Address perfectionism** | 完璧主義に対処: "Don't worry about making it pretty"
- **Emphasize learning** | 学習を強調: "The goal is to learn, not to impress"

---

### Slide 8: Stage 5: Test - Learning from Users | 段階5：テスト - ユーザーから学ぶ

#### Instructor Actions | インストラクターのアクション
- **Demonstrate think-aloud** | シンクアラウドを実演: Have a student use a prototype while thinking aloud
- **Show observation techniques** | 観察技法を表示: "Watch what they do, not what they say"
- **Practice feedback collection** | フィードバック収集を練習: "What questions should you ask?"

#### Key Points to Emphasize | 強調すべき要点
- **Don't help users** | ユーザーを助けない: Let them struggle - that's valuable data
- **Ask open-ended questions** | オープンエンド質問をする: "What was confusing?" not "Was this confusing?"
- **Observe behavior** | 行動を観察: Actions speak louder than words

#### Teaching Tips | 指導のヒント
- **Practice with students** | 学生と練習: "Let's test a simple prototype right now"
- **Show observation checklist** | 観察チェックリストを表示: What to watch for during testing
- **Emphasize iteration** | 反復を強調: "Testing is not the end - it's the beginning of improvement"

## Activity Transition | アクティビティ移行

### Before Starting Activity | アクティビティ開始前
1. **Ensure bot portal access** | ボットポータルアクセスを確保: All students can log in
2. **Distribute materials** | 教材を配布: Paper, pencils, any handouts
3. **Form pairs** | ペアを形成: Students work together but document individually
4. **Set up workspace** | ワークスペースをセットアップ: Clear desks, good lighting

### During Activity | アクティビティ中
1. **Circulate and observe** | 巡回して観察: Watch for students who need help
2. **Answer questions** | 質問に答える: But encourage them to try the bot first
3. **Provide encouragement** | 励ましを提供: "You're doing great - keep going"
4. **Monitor time** | 時間を監視: Help students stay on track

### Common Issues During Activity | アクティビティ中の一般的な問題

#### Students Skip Empathy Stage | 学生が共感段階をスキップ
- **Signs:** Jumping straight to solutions | ソリューションに直接飛ぶ
- **Intervention:** "Remember, we start with understanding users. What did you learn from your interviews?"
- **Bot Help:** Bot will remind them to complete empathy map

#### Problem Statements Too Vague | 問題文が曖昧すぎる
- **Signs:** "Students need a better app" | 「学生はより良いアプリが必要」
- **Intervention:** "Let's make this more specific. What exactly do students need? Why?"
- **Bot Help:** Bot provides POV template and examples

#### Students Get Stuck in Ideation | 学生が発想で行き詰まる
- **Signs:** Only 2-3 ideas generated | 2-3個のアイデアしか生成されない
- **Intervention:** "Try the 'worst possible idea' technique. What's the worst way to solve this?"
- **Bot Help:** Bot provides creative prompts and techniques

#### Prototypes Too Detailed | プロトタイプが詳細すぎる
- **Signs:** Spending 30+ minutes on one prototype | 1つのプロトタイプに30分以上費やす
- **Intervention:** "Remember, this is low-fidelity. Focus on the concept, not the details."
- **Bot Help:** Bot reminds about fidelity levels and time management

#### Testing Feedback Too Positive | テストフィードバックがポジティブすぎる
- **Signs:** "It's great!" "I love it!" | 「素晴らしい！」「気に入った！」
- **Intervention:** "Ask more specific questions. What was confusing? What would you change?"
- **Bot Help:** Bot provides observation checklist and probing questions
