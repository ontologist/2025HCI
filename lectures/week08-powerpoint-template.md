## Slide 3: What is Conversational UI?
**Title:** What is Conversational UI? | 会話型UIとは？

**Definition Box:**
Conversational User Interface (CUI) enables users to interact with computers using natural language through text or voice, mimicking human-to-human conversation.

**Key Characteristics:**
1. **Natural Language** | 自然言語
   - Users communicate in everyday language
2. **Turn-Taking** | ターンテイキング
   - Back-and-forth conversation flow
3. **Context Awareness** | コンテキスト認識
   - Remembers previous interactions
4. **Intent Recognition** | 意図認識
   - Understands what users want

**Why It Matters:**
- Accessibility: Natural for everyone
- Efficiency: Instant answers
- Availability: 24/7 support
- Scalability: Handle multiple users

## Slide 7: Dialog Design Principles
**Title:** Dialog Design Principles | 対話デザイン原則

**Five Principles:**

**1. Be Clear and Concise**
Bad: "We are currently experiencing a high volume..."
Good: "I'll be with you in about 2 minutes!"

**2. Set Clear Expectations**
"I can help you with order tracking, product info, and store hours."

**3. Provide Guidance**
Offer suggestions and quick reply buttons

**4. Confirm Understanding**
"Let me track order #12345. Is that correct?"

**5. Handle Errors Gracefully**
"I didn't understand. Could you rephrase, or speak with a team member?"

## Slide 8: Conversation Flow Structure
**Title:** Conversation Flow Structure | 会話フロー構造

**Flow Diagram:**

**1. Greeting** → Welcome and set expectations
**2. Intent Identification** → Understand user goal
**3. Information Gathering** → Collect necessary details
**4. Action & Response** → Provide answer/complete task
**5. Confirmation & Follow-Up** → Ensure satisfaction
**6. Closure** → End politely

**Example Flow:**
Bot: "Hi! I'm your campus assistant. What can I help you with?"
User: "Where is the library?"
Bot: "Which library? Main or Science?"
User: "Main Library"
Bot: "Main Library is in Building 5, open 8am-10pm today. Directions?"
User: "No, thanks"
Bot: "Great! Come back anytime!"

## Slide 10: Personality and Tone
**Title:** Personality and Tone in Chatbots | チャットボットのパーソナリティとトーン

**Why Personality Matters:**
- Builds trust
- Creates connection
- Brand alignment
- Differentiates experience

**Three Personality Dimensions:**

**1. Formal vs Casual**
Formal: "Good afternoon. How may I assist you?"
Casual: "Hey there! What can I help you with?"

**2. Professional vs Friendly**
Professional: "I'll process your request immediately."
Friendly: "I'm on it! Give me just a sec."

**3. Serious vs Playful**
Serious: "Your account balance is $523.45."
Playful: "You've got $523.45 in the bank! Nice!"

## Slide 11: Writing Effective Bot Responses
**Title:** Writing Effective Bot Responses | 効果的なボット応答の作成

**Five Best Practices:**

**1. Use Active Voice**
Passive: "Your order has been shipped."
Active: "We shipped your order!"

**2. Be Specific and Actionable**
Vague: "Soon"
Specific: "In 2 minutes"

**3. Show Empathy**
Without: "Payment failed. Try again."
With: "Oh no! Let's try another method together."

**4. Provide Options**
"I can't help with that directly, but I can:
- Connect you with a team member
- Send helpful resources
- Schedule a callback"

**5. Use Conversational Language**
Robotic: "Request acknowledged. Processing initiated."
Conversational: "Got it! Let me look that up."

## Slide 12: Button vs Free Text Input
**Title:** Button vs. Free Text Input | ボタン vs 自由テキスト入力

**When to Use Buttons:**
- Multiple choice questions
- Confirmation (Yes/No)
- Menu navigation
- Common actions

Advantages:
- Clear options
- Prevent errors
- Faster interaction

**When to Use Free Text:**
- Open-ended questions
- Description requests
- Feedback collection
- Custom queries

Advantages:
- Flexible input
- Natural expression
- Detailed information

**Hybrid Approach (Recommended):**
"How can I help you today?"
[Check Order] [Product Info] [Store Hours]
[Other - Type your question]

## Slide 13: Context and Memory
**Title:** Context and Memory in Conversations | 会話のコンテキストとメモリ

**Three Types of Context:**

**1. Session Context**
Within current conversation
Example: Remember "pizza" + "large"

**2. User Context**
Across sessions
Example: "Welcome back! Your usual order?"

**3. Environmental Context**
External factors
Example: Time, location, device

**Memory Best Practices:**
- Reference previous exchanges
- Avoid asking twice
- Handle session breaks gracefully
- "Welcome back! We were discussing..."
