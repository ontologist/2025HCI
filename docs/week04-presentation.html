What Are Personas? | „Éö„É´„ÇΩ„Éä„Å®„ÅØÔºü

Personas are fictional, yet realistic, representations of your key user segments based on research data.

Why Personas Matter

  • Focus design decisions - Design for specific users, not "everyone"
  • Build empathy - Make users real and memorable
  • Communicate insights - Share research findings with teams
  • Prevent feature creep - "Would Sarah use this?"

Anatomy of a Persona

Essential Components

1. Basic Information

  • Name
  • Photo
  • Demographics
  • Quote

2. Background & Context

  • Who they are
  • Their environment
  • Technology proficiency

3. Goals & Motivations

  • Primary goals
  • Motivations
  • Success criteria

4. Pain Points

  • Current challenges
  • Frustrations
  • Barriers

5. Behaviors & Patterns

  • How they work
  • Technology usage
  • Communication preferences

6. Needs & Requirements

  • From your research
  • Evidence-based
  • Priority ranking

Creating Data-Driven Personas

The Process

1. Review Research

Gather all interview notes and findings

2. Identify Segments

Look for patterns in behaviors and goals

3. Create 2-4 Personas

Each represents a segment

4. Make Them Real

Names, photos, authentic quotes

5. Validate

Every element traces to research

Persona Example

Sarah Chen - The Proactive Planner

Age: 21 | Year: Junior | Major: Business

Quote: "I need to see all my options at once so I can plan the perfect schedule around my part-time job."

Background

Junior business student working 20 hours/week. Highly organized, plans everything in advance. Comfortable with technology, expects apps to work quickly.

Goals

  • Register for classes that fit work schedule
  • Graduate on time with required courses
  • Maintain GPA while working

Pain Points

  • System doesn't show schedule conflicts
  • Must check each course individually
  • No way to compare with work schedule
  • Popular courses fill up within hours

Needs (from research)

  • Quickly see schedule conflicts
  • Compare course times with personal calendar
  • Save multiple schedule options
  • Receive notifications when courses open

Evidence: Based on interviews with 5 students, 4 mentioned work schedule conflicts as primary frustration.

User Journey Maps

Why Journey Maps Matter

  • Identify pain points - Where users struggle
  • Find opportunities - Where to improve experience
  • Build empathy - Walk in users' shoes
  • Align teams - Shared understanding
  • Prioritize features - Focus on high-impact moments

Journey Map Components

1-3. Persona, Scenario, Phases
  • Which user?
  • What goal?
  • 4-6 major stages
4-6. Actions, Touchpoints, Thoughts
  • What user does
  • Where interactions happen
  • What user is thinking
7-9. Emotions, Pain Points, Opportunities
  • How user feels
  • Where problems occur
  • Where to improve

Scenarios | „Ç∑„Éä„É™„Ç™

Narrative descriptions of how a persona uses your design to accomplish a goal

Types of Scenarios

Current State Scenario

  • How users accomplish tasks NOW
  • Highlights pain points
  • Based on research observations

Future State Scenario

  • How users will accomplish tasks with YOUR DESIGN
  • Shows improved experience
  • Guides design decisions

Writing Effective Scenarios

Scenario Structure

  1. Title: Descriptive and concise
  2. Persona: Which user?
  3. Context: Where, when, what situation?
  4. Goal: What are they trying to accomplish?
  5. Actions: Step-by-step walkthrough
  6. Outcome: Do they achieve the goal? How do they feel?

Best Practices

  • Be Specific: Include details like device, time, environment
  • Include Context: Time constraints, emotional state
  • Show Interactions: What user does, what system does
  • Highlight Emotions: How user feels at key moments
  • Base on Research: Use actual user quotes and behaviors

Using Personas in Design Decisions

The "Persona Check"

When making design decisions, ask:

  • "Would Sarah use this feature?" If no, why are we building it?
  • "How would Sarah discover this?" Is it obvious or hidden?
  • "Can Sarah do this on mobile during lunch?" Mobile context matters
  • "Does this solve Sarah's pain points?" Focus on real problems

Avoiding Anti-Patterns

Don't: Design for yourself, add features "because it's cool," ignore persona constraints

Do: Reference personas in every design review, test scenarios against wireframes, keep personas visible
