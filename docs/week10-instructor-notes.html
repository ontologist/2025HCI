    English Speaking Points:
    • "A good usability test starts with a solid plan. Let's break down the essential components."
    • "First: Test objectives. What do you want to learn? What design questions need answers?"
    • "For example: 'Can users successfully find and book an appointment?' or 'Is the chatbot's error handling effective?'"
    • "Second: Participant criteria. Who should you test with? They should match your target users."
    • "If you're designing a student planner app, test with students. If it's for elderly users, test with elderly people."
    • "Third: Test tasks. These are the scenarios users will perform. This is critical - bad tasks lead to bad data."
    • "Fourth: Test environment. Where will you test? What equipment do you need? What version of the prototype?"
    • "Fifth: Metrics. What will you measure? We'll cover this in detail shortly."
